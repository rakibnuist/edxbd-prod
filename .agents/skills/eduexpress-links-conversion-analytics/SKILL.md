# EduExpress Links, PR, Conversion and Analytics Plan

Implements Sections 14-16 of the Evidence-First SEO Plan.

## Backlink priorities (in order)

| Priority | Tactic | Target |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ask verified university partners to list EduExpress as official Bangladesh representative — only where true | 5-15 high-trust institutional links in 12 months |
| 2 | Publish original Bangladesh-China education data, cost benchmarks, annual outcome reports | Press, education sites, bloggers, AI citations |
| 3 | Run seminars with schools, colleges, alumni groups, universities; secure event-page links | Local authority + branded searches |
| 4 | Pitch expert commentary to credible Bangladesh media on policy changes, fraud prevention, choosing quality education | 1 meaningful earned-media mention/quarter |
| 5 | Collaborate with relevant YouTube educators and student communities; provide useful tools and sources | Referral traffic + brand demand, not link spam |
| 6 | Link-worthy tools: BDT cost calculator, EduFit comparison, deadline calendar, recognition checklist | Natural links + repeat visits |

## Never do

Bulk forum/comment/bookmark/web-2.0/directory packages; paid dofollow disguised as editorial; fake "top consultancy" lists controlled by EduExpress; exact-match anchor campaigns; claiming university partnership just because public applications exist; buying or gating Google reviews.

## AI search visibility

AI assistants surface brands they can retrieve and corroborate. The plan: SSR/SSG, consistent facts across sources, Bing + Google indexing, Q&A-shaped content, original data, third-party mentions, strong proof pages. llms.txt may be tested as a low-cost discovery aid — never present it as a guaranteed AI-ranking mechanism. Keep a monthly entity/AI visibility log: fixed prompts, date, location/account conditions, cited source.

## Primary conversion product: Free Education Fit Assessment

Collect: academic level/results, subject interest, budget, intake, language, career goal, preferred countries. Return a short written summary: suitable routes, risks, missing information, recommended counseling step.

## CRM pipeline (16 stages)

New lead → Contacted → Assessment scheduled → Assessment completed → Decision report delivered → Country/program shortlisted → Documents pending → Application submitted → Offer received → Visa preparation → Visa submitted → Visa approved/refused → Pre-departure → Enrolled → Alumni / review requested

## Lead-routing rules

| Trigger | Route |
|---|---|
| China page/interest | China team; show visa-first policy + relevant university context |
| Wave 1 country | Assigned active country owner — never untrained general sales staff |
| Launching country | Interest list + honest launch message; no payment/application promise until gate passes |
| High-risk profile | Senior counselor review before recommendations or payment |
| Medical program | Recognition/licensing review required |
| Sensitive documents | Secure upload; no WhatsApp-only archive as system of record; access logging + retention rules |
| Complaint/refund | Dedicated ticket, management owner, timestamped resolution path |

## Conversion events to track

assessment_start, assessment_submit, whatsapp_click, phone_click, appointment_booked, compare_started, compare_completed, cost_sheet_downloaded, university_view, success_story_view, application_started, offer_received, visa_outcome. Use UTM, landing page, destination, counselor fields in CRM.

## Measurement stack

GSC (indexing, queries, CWV, structured-data errors); GA4 (quality conversions, attribution, journeys); Bing Webmaster (crawl + ChatGPT-search discovery); GBP (calls, clicks, directions, search terms); CRM (response time, assessment completion, application rate, offer rate, visa outcome by country, refund/complaint records); rank tracker (branded, local, China, country clusters — Bangladesh and Dhaka separately); monthly entity/AI visibility log.

## KPI targets (management planning goals, NOT guarantees)

| Period | SEO/visibility | Trust/conversion | Operations |
|---|---|---|---|
| 90 days | All P0 closed; priority pages indexed; first China cluster growth; local visibility improving | Transparency pages live; 20+ authentic new reviews target; assessment tracked | China offer terms, CMS verification, Wave 1 owners operational |
| 6 months | Page-one progress for core China queries; 3,000+ qualified organic visits/month; 8-10 quality referring domains/month | 100+ total genuine GBP reviews; rising branded searches + assessment conversion | China hub mature; Wave 1 active with refreshed data |
| 12 months | Multiple destination clusters ranking; 10,000+ qualified organic visits/month | Strong independent mentions; repeat/referral leads; annual transparency report | Wave 2 live; Wave 3 only where gates passed |
| 18-24 months | National top-tier visibility for education consultancy searches | Large corpus of verified stories/reviews; clear public differentiation | Reliable country teams, data governance, ongoing refresh |

## Monthly report contents

Report leads and revenue-quality outcomes, not traffic alone: non-branded clicks, top landing pages, local actions, assessment completion, qualified lead rate, applications, offers, visa outcomes by destination, refund/complaint trends, review growth, content freshness, next-month actions.
