Executive Decision
EduExpress should not enter the Bangladesh market as another consultancy advertising scholarships, “easy visa,” large partner counts and generic success-rate claims. Those messages are already common. The stronger market gap is verified education advice, visible fee transparency and written evidence in a sector where students and families increasingly fear misleading promises, lost money and misuse of personal documents. [S8-S12]
Chosen market position
Bangladesh's Evidence-First Education Consultancy
Public brand promise
Better Education. Clear Costs. Written Proof.
Campaign line
Compare Before You Commit.
China offer - approved wording
No file opening charge. No EduExpress service fee before China visa approval. Any university, embassy, medical, translation, courier, deposit or other third-party fee required earlier must be itemized in writing before the student proceeds.
Why the wording matters: “All payment after visa” is powerful only when it is literally true. Some universities or official processes may require application fees, deposits or other payments earlier. The website must distinguish EduExpress charges from third-party charges so the promise remains honest and defensible.

1. Bangladesh Market Reality and Competitive White Space
1.1 What competitors already say
Bangladesh consultancies commonly promote free counseling, no file-opening fee, scholarship support, payment or service charge after visa, large partner networks and high visa-success claims. Therefore, “no file opening charge,” “transparent” and “pay after visa” are useful conversion features, but none is a defensible category-level USP by itself. Examples include Admission Connect and Vidya International, alongside many other agencies using similar language. [S6-S7]
Common market message
Why it is weak as a standalone USP
EduExpress response
Free consultation
Expected entry offer; almost everyone can copy it.
Make the consultation produce a useful Education Fit Summary.
No file opening fee
Strong reassurance but already used by competitors.
Keep it, then add written cost separation and milestone rules.
Pay after visa
Reduces risk, but wording is often vague.
Make it China-specific, define “EduExpress service fee,” list exceptions and publish terms.
Scholarship specialist
Crowded and can make education quality look secondary.
Sell best-fit education and treat scholarship as one affordability factor.
98% / 100% success
Often unverifiable and increasingly distrusted.
Publish scoped, period-based statistics only when auditable.
150+ partners
Does not show whether universities are suitable or relationships are direct.
Show partner type, evidence, last verification and student-fit rationale.
Honest and transparent
Easy to claim, difficult to prove.
Productize transparency through ClearCost, Proof Pack, receipt rules and public policies.
1.2 The trust crisis creates an opportunity
Recent alleged overseas-education fraud cases in Bangladesh involved large sums and hundreds of students, while the UGC has warned about unauthorized foreign-university operations. A Financial Times investigation also highlighted overseas recruitment agencies sharing student passport details online. These events create a market where proof, privacy and payment control can outperform louder promises. [S8-S12]
Strategic inference
The open market position is not “the agency with the most scholarships.” It is “the agency that lets families see the evidence, total cost, risks and responsibility before committing money.”

Internal links to country pillar, university profiles, service terms and related guides.

18.1 Decision rules for scaling
Do not add another country because a competitor has it; add it when EduExpress can verify and service it better.
Do not increase content volume if pages are stale or unsupported; fix accuracy first.
Do not use a China success statistic on another destination.
Do not accept payment without a written purpose, recipient, milestone and refund status.
Do not publish student documents or identifiable visa pages.
Do not chase backlinks until the linked page is genuinely useful and current.
